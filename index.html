Introduction

The ACL (Anterior Cruciate Ligament) injury is a common sports injury. Recovery requires a structured plan to restore strength, stability, and mobility. This guide breaks down each phase of recovery.

Month 1-2: Early Mobility

Goals:

  • Regain range of motion in the knee
  • Reduce swelling and inflammation
  • Initiate light strengthening exercises safely

Exercises:

  • Straight Leg Raises: Lie flat, lift your operated leg to 45 degrees, and lower it. Repeat for 3 sets of 10.
  • Quad Sets: Tighten your quadriceps muscle and hold for 5 seconds. Repeat for 3 sets of 10-15.
  • Ankle Pumps: Flex and point your toes to improve circulation. Perform for 2-3 minutes.

Month 3-4: Building Strength

Goals:

  • Strengthen key muscles
  • Enhance knee stability

Exercises:

  • Mini Squats: Bend knees to 30-45 degrees and hold for 2 seconds. Repeat for 3 sets of 10-15.
  • Single-Leg Balance: Stand on the operated leg on an unstable surface for 15-30 seconds. Perform 3 sets.
  • Step-Ups: Step onto a low step with the operated leg and step back down. Repeat for 3 sets of 10.
